Getting Paid — Renascent Payment Rules

Follow these rules exactly to avoid short payment on your final invoice

⚠️
Your final invoice must match the authorization sheet exactly. The claim payments department cannot add anything after the fact — go back to your adjuster before submitting if anything changes.
Final Invoice Checklist
✓All labour dollar amounts match the auth sheet and clearly state what each labour line is for
✓All part numbers, quantities, and prices match exactly
✓Sublets are booked out on your invoice
✓If you need to add parts or labour, go back to your adjuster to amend the claim FIRST
✓Include any sublet invoices when submitting for payment

Appearance Protection Claims

Exterior paint, rust, corrosion, and interior damage — these use a separate email and process from mechanical claims

⚠️
Different email than mechanical claims! All appearance protection claims go to appearanceclaims@rps-tpa.com — not claims@rps-tpa.com. Using the wrong address will delay or reject the claim.
📸
Module photos required for all corrosion/rust/paint bubbling claims. In addition to the standard vehicle photos, you must include: module installation location, black & red wire connection, and green wire connection. Missing these photos = rejected claim.
🎨 Exterior Paint Damage

Permanent paint damage caused by environmental contaminants on outer body sheet metal panels.

What's Covered
✦Acid rain damage
✦Tree sap
✦Bird droppings
✦Road tar & salt
✦Overspray & insects
✦Industrial fall-out, oxidation & loss of gloss
Areas Covered
Roof · Rear quarter panels · Front fenders · Doors (exterior, closed position) · Engine hood (exterior, closed position) · Trunk lid / tailgate (exterior, closed position)
📋 Requires: Exterior Paint Damage Claim Report + module photos
🫧 Paint Bubbling

A paint bubble on outer body sheet metal where no rust or bare metal is visible and paint is not broken around the bubble.

Key Requirement
✦No surface rust or bare metal showing
✦Paint must NOT be broken, cracked, split, chipped, peeled, delaminated, flaked, or missing around or leading up to the bubble
Areas Covered
Roof · Rear quarter panels · Front fenders · Doors (exterior, closed) · Engine hood (exterior, closed) · Trunk lid / tailgate (exterior, closed)
📋 Requires: Corrosion Damage Claim Report + module photos
🟤 Cosmetic Rust

Surface rust damage where paint is missing and the rust area is at least 2 cm in diameter.

Areas Covered
Outer body sheet metal panels visible when walking around the vehicle:
Roof · Rear quarter panels · Front fenders · Doors (exterior, closed) · Engine hood (exterior, closed) · Trunk lid / tailgate (exterior, closed)
📋 Requires: Corrosion Damage Claim Report + module photos
🕳️ Rust Perforation (Hole)

A hole caused by rust or corrosion through finished painted body sheet metal panels, frame, or floorboards. A visible hole must be confirmed upon inspection to qualify.

Areas Covered
Roof · Rear quarter panels · Front fenders · Doors · Rocker panels · Engine hood · Trunk lid / tailgate · Frame · Floorboards
🔍 Must visually confirm a hole is present before filing — not just surface rust
📋 Requires: Corrosion Damage Claim Report + module photos
🪑 Interior Appearance Damage

Note: This is the Appearance Protection version of interior coverage — different from the Hazard Protect rip/tear/burn coverage. Uses a separate form and email.

What's Covered
✦
Permanent stains from accidental spills — food, beverages, oil, grease, urine, or similar liquids
✦
Rips & tears less than 12 cm in length (accidental only)
✦
Punctures & burns up to 1 cm in diameter (accidental only)
Areas Covered
✦Seats (vinyl/leather and fabric)
✦Armrests
✦Headrests
✦Carpets
✦Vinyl/leather dashboard
📋 Requires: Interior Damage Claim Report (no module photos needed)

Defective Rust Module Procedure

When the rust protection module itself fails — separate from a damage claim

✦Modules are warranted 5 years from installation date
✦WRC-administered contracts: warranted for the full contract term (up to 10 years if inspections are maintained)
✦Dealer's responsibility: confirm customer warranty is valid before replacing the module

How to Submit a Claim

Step-by-step process for Strategic DX (SDX) — the preferred submission platform

⭐
Important: After setting up your SDX login, email Michelle Jawan to confirm. There is a function in SDX that allows all stores to see each other's claims — she needs to know when you've successfully created a login to switch this on for your account.
Step-by-Step Claim Process
1
Open the Work Order
Document the date, customer info, vehicle info, odometer, and 3 C's (Complaint, Cause, Correction).
2
Prepare Your Estimate
Build estimate with part numbers, Mitchell ProDemand labour times, and applicable rates before calling.
3
Obtain Prior Authorization
Submit through tricor.yde.claims using your dealer number, or call 1-844-601-2018 during business hours.
4
Complete Authorized Repairs
Perform only what was authorized. If additional parts or labour are needed, contact your adjuster to amend before proceeding.
5
Match Invoice to Auth Sheet
Every line on your invoice must match the authorization exactly — amounts, part numbers, quantities.
6
Submit Signed Invoice for Payment
Email signed customer invoice + any sublet invoices to claimpayments@rps-tpa.com or fax to (614) 652-3684.
